About

Qosim is a passionate legal researcher, an aspirant to the bar, and a technology enthusiast. A graduate in Common and Islamic Law from Bayero University Kano and an Associate Member of both the Institute of Mediators and Conciliators (ICMC) and the Institute of Islamic Banking and Finance (ACIBF), Qosim has cultivated expertise in litigation, alternative dispute resolution, and Islamic finance, including Shari’ah-compliant financial systems (including Islamic capital markets), inheritance law, and endowment frameworks.

He has honed his practical skills through multiple internships at top-tier law firms and legal institutions, gaining experience in legal research, drafting, public interest litigation, corporate governance, and telecommunications law. His leadership within the university community and beyond includes serving as Attorney-General of Solace Chambers after serving in several positions of the Chambers; Director of Pre-Trial at the Bayero University Kano Law Clinic; and the Director of Research at the National ADR Society, among others, where he fosters mentorship, research, and professional development among aspiring lawyers.

In addition to his legal pursuits, Qosim is a web developer and digital strategist, creating responsive platforms that support legal publications, mentorship initiatives, and collaborative learning. His work reflects a commitment to bridging knowledge, technology, and law to advance legal scholarship and practice.

Driven by intellectual curiosity and a dedication to excellence, Qosim continues to explore innovative solutions in legal research, education, and professional development, striving to make legal knowledge accessible and actionable for students, practitioners, and the broader community.

Legal and Academic Research

Conducting structured legal research and scholarly analysis across diverse areas of law, with particular attention to doctrinal interpretation, legal theory, and the systematic evaluation of judicial decisions. Litigation Research Assistance

Providing analytical support in litigation by conducting case law reviews, statutory interpretation, and examination of applicable legal authorities. This process involves assisting in the development of well-grounded legal arguments, identifying persuasive precedents, and contributing research insights that strengthen litigation strategies and legal submissions.

Islamic Finance & Shari’ah Governance

Engaging in research and analytical study of the legal and regulatory structures governing Islamic finance and Shari’ah-compliant financial systems. This includes examining principles such as risk-sharing, asset-backed financing, and ethical financial practices, while exploring the institutional frameworks that ensure compliance with Shari’ah governance standards.

Legal and Legislative Drafting

Preparing and reviewing legal documents, policy drafts, and legislative materials with attention to clarity, structure, and legal precision. This competency involves understanding the principles of legislative drafting, the formulation of coherent legal provisions, and the articulation of ideas in language suitable for legal and policy contexts.
